The Drake Equation

A mathematical formula for estimating the number of active, communicative extraterrestrial civilizations in our galaxy

N = R* × fp × ne × fl × fi × fc × L

N

The number of civilizations with which we could potentially communicate

R*

The average rate of star formation in our galaxy (estimated: 1-3 stars per year)

fp

The fraction of stars that have planetary systems (estimated: 0.5-1)

ne

The average number of planets that could potentially support life per star with planets (estimated: 0.1-5)

fl

The fraction of planets that could support life that actually develop life (estimated: 0.1-1)

fi

The fraction of planets with life that develop intelligent life (estimated: 0.1-1)

fc

The fraction of civilizations that develop technology that releases detectable signs of their existence (estimated: 0.1-1)

L

The length of time such civilizations release detectable signals (estimated: 100-10,000,000 years)

Modern Insights

  • Recent exoplanet discoveries suggest fp and ne might be higher than originally estimated
  • The discovery of extremophiles on Earth suggests fl might be higher than previously thought
  • SETI and other programs are actively searching for fc signals
  • L remains the most uncertain factor in the equation
